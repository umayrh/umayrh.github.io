Introduction

  • Individuation has a principle

By page 2, Simondon deems philosophy - atomism and hylomorphism, in particular - to have inadequately described individuation. And so Simondon folds into philosophy concepts from thermodynamics like:

  • potential energy
  • phase

And, at least, in the first half of the book, his examples focus on:

  • crystallization

This lead to a redefinition of ontogenesis - not in terms of the constituted individual - but as a relationship of being and phase-shifting, a re-consideration of being, and a revaluation of the logic of being:

  • ‚Äúwe must consider being not as substance or matter or form but as a tense, supersaturated system above the level of unity‚Äù
  • ‚ÄúUnity, which is characteristic of the individual being, and identity, which authorizes the usage of the principle of the excluded middle, do not apply to pre-individual being‚Äù
